<p><p>Airlaid machines are utilized in producing non-woven fabrics through various bonding techniques. Latex or spray bonding involves applying adhesive to bind fibers, creating a strong, flexible product. Thermal bonding uses heat to fuse fibers together, resulting in durable materials. Hydrogen bonding harnesses moisture in the fibers to promote adhesion, enhancing softness. Other methods may include mechanical bonding or chemical processes. Each technique caters to specific applications, influencing the market dynamics and product characteristics in the airlaid sector.</p></p>
