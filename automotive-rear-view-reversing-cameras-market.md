<p><p>The automotive rear-view reversing cameras market primarily consists of two types: CCD (Charge-Coupled Device) cameras and CMOS (Complementary Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor) cameras. CCD cameras are known for their superior image quality, low light performance, and color accuracy, making them ideal for high-end vehicles. In contrast, CMOS cameras are more compact, cost-effective, and consume less power. They offer adequate performance for most applications and can integrate advanced features like higher frame rates and digital processing, catering to a broader range of vehicles.</p></p>
