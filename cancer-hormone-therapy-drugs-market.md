<p><p>Cancer hormone therapy drugs are classified into several types based on their mechanisms of action. Tamoxifen is a selective estrogen receptor modulator used primarily in breast cancer. Aromatase inhibitors block estrogen production, effective in estrogen receptor-positive breast cancers. Antiandrogens inhibit male hormone effects, useful in prostate cancer. Androgen synthesis inhibitors reduce hormone production in the adrenal glands, also targeting prostate cancer. Other market categories include drugs with different hormonal targets or mechanisms, addressing various hormone-dependent cancers.</p></p>
