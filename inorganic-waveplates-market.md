<p><p>Inorganic waveplates are optical devices designed to manipulate the polarization of light. The 1/4 waveplate introduces a phase shift of 90 degrees, converting linearly polarized light into circularly polarized light or vice versa, making it essential in applications like lasers and imaging systems. The 1/2 waveplate, with a 180-degree phase shift, is used to rotate the polarization direction of light without changing its intensity. Both types find applications in telecommunications, microscopy, and optical instrumentation, catering to diverse market needs.</p></p>
