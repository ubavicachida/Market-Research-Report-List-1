<p><p>The petroleum (PET) coke market is primarily divided into two types: calcined coke and fuel grade coke. Calcined coke is produced by heating green petroleum coke in a furnace, resulting in a carbon-rich material essential for aluminum production and anode manufacturing in steelmaking. Fuel grade coke, on the other hand, is used primarily as a low-cost fuel source in power generation and cement manufacturing, due to its high calorific value and lower ash content. Both types serve distinct industrial applications.</p></p>
