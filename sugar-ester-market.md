<p><p>The sugar ester market is categorized into monoesters, diesters, and triesters based on the number of fatty acid chains attached to the sugar molecule. Monoesters consist of one fatty acid chain, which is useful for emulsification and stabilization in food products. Diesters contain two fatty acid chains, enhancing their role as surfactants and in cosmetics. Triesters have three fatty acid chains, providing a richer texture and greater film-forming abilities, making them ideal for food and personal care applications.</p></p>
